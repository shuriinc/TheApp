Imports
Import a Spreadsheet

Get your files ready

If you’re on a mobile device you’ll need to have Google Drive, DropBox or other file-sharing app already downloaded to access your spreadsheet.

Choose where your data will go

Once you’ve chosen a spreadsheet, you’ll be asked to create the name of your new Group. A Group is just what it sounds like: a way to group together similar information from your Databases into one place. Groups automatically get created with every import.

Below that, choose which Database you’d like to import your contacts into. They will be imported into both your chosen Group and Database.



You can only import into existing Databases that you either own or have editing permissions on. So if you want to import your contacts into a new Database, make sure to create one before you start.

Map Your Columns

Next, start mapping how you want your spreadsheet to look in Shuri, one column at a time.

Shuri will pull up a column from your spreadsheet and show you samples from its rows. You can click Skip if you don’t want that column imported or:

Choose which kind of item it is

Select if this column refers to a Person, an Org or a Touch.

Select the appropriate field

Choose which field this info refers to (ie. First Name, Last Name, Business Phone Number, etc…) and click Next.

Create a Custom Field

If none of Shuri’s fields are close enough to the type of data in your column, you can create a custom field by selecting None of the Above.

Once you’ve titled it, you’ll need to select what type of field it is (ie. anything, anything (multiple lines), date, number, phone, etc...)

Create Custom Tags

Under None of the Above you can also create tags. Tags can be used to label and categorize your data.

When you select to create tags in importing, it’s most likely because your column has many occurrences of only a few different entries (ie. in a spreadsheet of US Senators, a column that denotes political party).

You create a name for your tag set, which becomes the umbrella that these tags live under, and the entries in the column become the tags for the Person, Org, and/or Touch in their row of the spreadsheet.

Check your work

Once you’ve mapped out all of your desired columns, you’ll find a summary page where you can go back and make changes by swiping left on any of the fields and clicking the pencil.

Finish the Import

Once you’re ready, click Import. It may take up to a couple minutes for the import to complete, depending on the size of your spreadsheet.

When importing is over, you’ll see all of those People Orgs and/or Touches stored in the Database and Group you chose.

Every item that was linked in your spreadsheet will be linked in Shuri (ie. associated People will show up in the People tab on an Org’s page and vice-versa).

To fill in more information about these contacts, see FLESH OUT YOUR DATA.
